About The Event

About the Millenium Expo

In just a few Earth weeks, Earth will complete its 3000th revolution around the Sun in the Common Era. To celebrate the new millenia, the International Science Commission is holding a 3-day science convention in Beijing, China. Virtual attendance will be available throughout the Solar System for those unable to arrive in-person.

Where

China National Convention Center, Beijing China

When

Jan 1 - 3, 3000
(Earth Coordinated Time)

Event Schedule

Here is our event schedule

  • Day 1
  • Day 2
  • Day 3

All times listed are in China Standard Time. Virtual attendees must adjust for time-zone and light-speed differences where necessary.

07:00 AM

Registration

In-person attendees can aquire all necessary materials in the Chien-Shiung Conference Room starting at 7:00 AM. The registration desk will be open starting at 7:00 AM each morning.

10:00 AM

Keynote. Suma Adebayo

A member of the board of the International Science Commission and the Millenium Expo Director delivers the conference's Keynote speech.

11:00 AM

Shor's Algorithm and the Future of Cryptography. Marie Thomas

One of the solar system's leading experts in quantum computing discusses the fate of encryption in the near future.

12:00 AM

Progress on The Reimann Hypothesis. Dr. Cole Emmerich

Topics include Modular Forms, Turing Computability, Primality Testing, and Vertex Operator Algebras.

02:00 PM

Life in Space. Dr. Jack Christiansen

Astrobiology PhD Jack Christiansen outlines the future of life outside the Heliosphere and discusses the recent biosignatures discovered by the KRAM Space Telescope

03:00 PM

The Dyson Sphere. Alejandrin Littel

From the same company who manufactures the nanotubes used in the Earth-Moon and Mars-Phobos Space Tethers comes an exciting new discovery.

04:00 PM

Terraforming Gas Giants. Willow Trantow

Ecological technology has been used to artifically cool the Earth, transform Mars and Venus, and may soon allow humans to inhabit the outer planets as well.

10:00 AM

How I Found A Planet. Ryan O'Leary

Astrophysicist and Engineer Ryan O'Leary recounts how he helped land an unmanned spacecraft on Planet 9.

10:30 AM

Artificial Intelligence Pt. 1 Dr. Cole Emmerich

In Part 1 of this three-hour lecture, Dr. Emmerich reviews Matrix Transformations, Nonlinear Dynamics, and Perturbation Theory for Neural Networks.

01:00 PM

Artificial Intelligence Pt. 2 Dr. Cole Emmerich

In Part 2 of this three-hour lecture, Dr. Emmerich builds on his Award-winning work on the efficacy, decidability, and completeness of convolutional neural networks.

02:30 PM

What Space Travel is Really Like. Sofia Lebedev

Astronaut Sofia Lebedev shares the story of her 70-year journey to the Kuiper Belt and back, the furthest and longest manned space travel mission in human history.

03:00 PM

A Timeline of Technological Advancement. Spencer Williams

Renowned Science Historian Spencer Williams reminsces on the past, and makes a few predictions about humanity's future.

04:00 PM

Nuclear Energy and the Kardashev Scale. Dr. Zachary Igwe

Energy expert Zachary Igwe suggests that humanity's favorite energy source may not be ideal for Kardashev Type I+ civilizations.

10:00 AM

How to Cure a Prion Disease. Dr. Holokai Palakiko

Misfolded proteins have been killing humans for centuries. Recent breakthroughs with synthetic life may offer a miraculous cure.

11:00 AM

Quantum Tunneling and FLT. Dr. Jayden Maxwell

Although faster-than-light travel is impossible, Quantum Tunneling may allow light-speed communication with near infinite range using less energy than an LED lightbulb.

12:00 AM

CRISPR and Agriculture: A Match Made in Heaven. Brock & Derek Jarocha

The founders of Earth's largest producer of carbohydrates and the engineers of the Solar System's most grown crop detail the genetic technology at the heart of it all.

02:00 PM

Martian Archeology and Non-Earth Life. Dr. Cho Takahashi

Astrogeologist Dr. Cho Takahashi may have found proof of non-Earth life trapped in Martian rock in the Argyre Basin.

03:00 PM

Closing Ceremony. Suma Adebayo

Suma Adebayo returns to review the presentations given over the past three days and close out the Solar System's first interplanetary science fair.

F.A.Q

  • Are tickets limited?

    Although the China National Conference Center is quite large, capacity is limited and in-person tickets may sell out. Virtual capacity is unlimited however and those wishing to attend virtually will not be prohibited from doing so.

  • When are tickets available?

    Tickets are available for purchase from now until Dec 31, 2999 for in-person attendees, while virtual tickets will be availble until the end of the conference.

  • Do I need to be affiliated with a University or corporation to register?

    No. Conference registration is open to everyone, although certain organizations may provide exclusive discounts or events during the conference for those that are affiliated.

  • Is there an age or academic requirement to register?

    Tickets must be purchased by legal adults, but attendance is open to all registered individuals regardless of age. There are also no academic requirements, although some presented material will be very high-level and may not be accesible to those unfamiliar.

  • When is the venue open?

    The China National Convention Center will be open 24 hours a day, although the Chien-Shiung Conference Room will be closed from 10:00 PM to 5:00 AM each night.
    For information on when specific events will be occuring throughout the Expo, see the event schedule.

  • How can I attend virtually?

    Upon purchasing a ticket for virtual attendance, a link to a portal will be shared with you. After 7:00 AM on January 1st, virtual attendees can connect their telescreens to their registration name using this link and will be able to watch the lectures and tour the China National Convention Center via cameras set up throughout the Expo.
